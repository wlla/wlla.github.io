Audio Content

Audio must have a transcript. The benefits of providing transcripts are numerous. They are essential for someone who is Deaf or Deaf-Blind. They can be used by someone just learning the language of the content. They allow all users to be multi-modal in their consumption of the content. They allow for text search of the content. They improve access to scholars. They improve SEO. I'll stop there, but transcripts are great.

Prerecorded Audio

Prerecorded audio are required to have transcripts. A transcript identifies speakers and contains text of all the speech and meaningful sound. Meaningful sounds include sound effects, clapping, laughter, etc.

You can present a transcript as plain text in webpage or in a document. Time-stamped transcripts can be presented in a variety of formats including WebVTT.

Transcription Tools

Technology for computer generated transcription is improving every day. It's starting to be incorporated into more products, Google Chat for example.

CADET - Caption And Description Editing Tool

Free tool for creating writing captions and video description tracks. Tool will let you know estimated speech time for text so you can write scripts to fit into a tracks quite periods. Once you write video description, you will want to narrate it or turn it into computer synthesized speech tracks outside of the tool.

Audio Controls

Avoid having media that automatically plays. If you do have audio that automatically plays, you'll need to follow level AA success criteria: WCAG 2.1 1.4.2 Audio Control.

This success criteria requires that if you have audio that plays more than 3 seconds, a user must be able to pause or stop the audio, or they must be able to control the audio volume indepentently of their device's overall volume.

Low or No Background Audio

WCAG 2.1 1.4.7 Low or No Background Audio is a level AAA success criteria applying to certain audio-only content. This content must be:

  1. Audio-only
  2. Primarily speech
  3. Not an audio CAPTCHA or logo
  4. Not musical in nature

For this type of audio content, the success criteria says that either:

  • There is no background noise.
  • You can turn off the background noise.
  • The background noise is 20 decibels lower, or about 4x quieter, than the foreground speech.
